Raspberry cheesecake

This cheesecake recipe comes from Gordon Ramsay. It's a baked cheesecake without crust, supposed to be a New York classic. I made some other types of New York cheesecakes before. Personally I think the key is to fully beat the cream cheese and not to over-cook. Having an

electric mixer will definitely save a lot of hard work, but whisk by hand is totally achievable too. Try to use fresh raspberry instead of frozen ones, the soursweet taste is perfect compliment for the creamy cheesecake.

Step 1

Preheat the oven to 180 degree, and prepare the cake mold. Butter the mold using thewrapping of butter is a good saving idea. Cut a round parchment paper for the base.

Step 2

Soften the cream cheese in room temperature, then add the sugar and whisk in the mixer (about 8-10 mins). Beat eggs in a bowl, then add bit by bit into the cheese mixture to ensure fully combine after each addition.

Step 3

Add the flour and mix well, going through a sieve can help to avoid any lump. Then add zest of one lemon, and fold in the raspberries. Try to be careful and not to break the berries. Bake in the oven for 35-40 mins, until the edge is separated from the mold and the top is still a bit moist. Try not to over bake your cheesecake.
